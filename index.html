Welcome To Latitude With Attitude


An Analysis of Weather and the Equator


In school we are taught that "it is hot at the equator" and as you move away from the equator, average temperatures decrease. Through a single-day analysis of temperatures, humidity, cloudiness and wind speed in over 500 randomly-selected cities, actual differences in weather are not as pronounced as may be assumed.

This site is dedicated to an anlysis of weather changes in relation to a city's distance from the equator. To accomplish this analysis, I tapped the OpenWeatherMap API and assembled a dataset of over 500 cities.

After assembling the dataset in a Pandas dataframe, I utilizied Matplotlib to create plots highlighting the relationship of each city's maximum temperature, humidity, cloudiness and windspeed to its latitude.

In this site you'll find visualizations demonstrating the relationship of latitude to each of these weather effects, and a brief analysis of each.
